The 67th Annual Grammy Awards was a historic night for numerous artists.

By Gracie Kwon

Beyoncé

Beyoncé's 27-song album Cowboy Carter won Album of the Year. Released on March 29, 2024, this album now puts the artist at the top of the leaderboard with 35 Grammy awards, breaking the record for most awards and nominations ever.

Kendrick Lamar

Kendrick Lamar's song "Not Like Us" received five different awards:
  1. Record of the Year
  2. Song of the Year
  3. Best Rap Performance
  4. Best Rap Song
  5. Best Music Video

The Beatles

The Beatles won Best Rock Performance for their song "Now and Then." However, this song made history in a different way as well.

This award marks the first winner to use artificial intelligence in their song. The late Lennon's voice was extracted from a demo dating back to the 1970s and then incorporated into the newer version of the single.

With this, the group pushes the norm of music, opening the door to other similar uses of artifical intelligence for other artists moving forward.
